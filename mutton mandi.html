Dish Name: Mutton Mandi

ingredients:

  • Prepare Arabic Mandi Masala:

    • Coriander seeds: 1½ tablespoons
    • Cinnamon sticks: 4-5
    • Green cardamom: 12-15
    • Black peppercorns: 1 teaspoon
    • Cumin seeds: ½ tablespoon
    • Cloves: 9-10
    • Dried lemon: ½
    • Nutmeg: ½ piece
    • Saffron strands: ½ teaspoon
    • Bay leaves: 2
    • Himalayan pink salt: ½ teaspoon or to taste
    • Turmeric powder: ½ teaspoon
    • Red chilli powder: ½ teaspoon or to taste
  • Prepare Mandi:

    • Cooking oil: 2 tablespoons
    • Onion (chopped): 1 medium
    • Cinnamon sticks: 2
    • Star anise: 1
    • Green cardamom: 5-6
    • Black peppercorns: 8-10
    • Cloves: 3-4
    • Ginger garlic paste: 1½ tablespoons
    • Water: 5 cups or as required
    • Stock cube: 1-2 (optional)
    • Cooking oil: 1 tablespoon
    • Mutton whole leg (approx. 1.25 kg)
    • Himalayan pink salt: 1 teaspoon or to taste
  • Prepare Mandi Rice:

    • Cooking oil: 3 -4 tablespoons
    • Cloves: 2
    • Green cardamom: 2
    • Bay leaves: 2
    • Dried lemons: 2
    • Onion (chopped): 1 small
    • Basmati rice (Falak Extreme): 600g
    • Water: as required
    • Himalayan pink salt: 1 teaspoon or to taste
    • Charcoal: for smoke
    • Fried almonds (for garnish)

Directions:

  1. Prepare Arabic Mandi Masala:

    1. In a spice grinder, add coriander seeds, cinnamon sticks, green cardamom, black peppercorns, cumin seeds, cloves, dried lemon, nutmeg, saffron strands, and bay leaves. Grind well to make a powder.
    2. Add Himalayan pink salt, turmeric powder, and red chilli powder, then grind again. Arabic mandi masala is ready!

  2. Prepare Mandi:

    1. In a large pot, heat cooking oil and add chopped onion. Mix well until soft.
    2. Add cinnamon sticks, star anise, green cardamom, black peppercorns, and cloves. Mix well.
    3. Add ginger garlic paste, mix well, and sauté for 1-2 minutes.
    4. Add 1 teaspoon of the prepared Arabic mandi masala and pink salt, then mix well. Cook for 1 minute.
    5. Prick the mutton leg using a fork on both sides.
    6. In the pot, add the mutton leg, water, and stock cube. Mix well and bring to a boil.
    7. Remove any scum that rises to the top, flip the mutton leg, cover, and cook on low flame until the mutton is 90% tender (about 40-45 minutes).
    8. Once done, take out the mutton leg and set it aside. Strain the stock (about 2½ cups) and reserve for later use.
    9. Sprinkle 1-2 teaspoons of the prepared Arabic mandi masala and a little cooking oil on both sides of the mutton leg, then rub evenly.
    10. Grill the mutton leg in a preheated oven at 180°C for 10 minutes on both sides (using both upper and lower grills).

  3. Prepare Mandi Rice:

    1. In a bowl, add rice and water. Wash thoroughly, soak for 30 minutes, then strain the soaked rice and set aside.
    2. In a pot, heat cooking oil and add cloves, green cardamom, bay leaves, dried lemons, and onion. Sauté until light golden.
    3. Add the soaked rice and 2 teaspoons of the prepared Arabic mandi masala. Mix well.
    4. Add the reserved mutton stock (2½ cups) and mix well.
    5. Add Himalayan pink salt (adjust as needed) and mix again. Bring the mixture to a boil, and cook on medium flame until about ½ cup of water is left.
    6. Cover the pot and steam cook on low flame for 10-12 minutes until the rice is tender.

  4. Final Touch:

    1. On a serving platter, place the mandi rice and grilled mutton leg.
    2. Give the dish a coal smoke for 2 minutes to enhance the flavor.
    3. Garnish with fried almonds and serve with salad and tomato chutney!
